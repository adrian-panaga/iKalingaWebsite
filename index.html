About iKalinga SMS

Built for the communities that need it most

iKalinga SMS was born from a Quality Improvement (QI) initiative to close the persistent communication gap between local health centers and the communities they serve.

🧐 What is it?

A secure, offline-compatible C# desktop application that allows authorized barangay health workers to send targeted SMS broadcasts to community residents. It manages an encrypted resident database and sends messages through a local USB GSM modem — with no internet connection required.

👥 Who made it?

Developed by nursing students at the School of Nursing and Allied Health & Behavioral Sciences (SONAHBS) as a QI capstone project. Designed for use by Barangay Health Workers (BHWs) and health center staff — people who may not have advanced technical backgrounds.

📍 Where is it used?

Currently piloted at the City Camp District Health Center in Baguio City, Cordillera Administrative Region (CAR), Philippines — a center that serves approximately 100 patients per day across 14 barangays. Designed to work at any similar community health center.

📅 When did it start?

The project was formally proposed on October 29, 2025, following a needs assessment at City Camp District Health Center. The assessment — led by Head Nurse Mr. Andrew Nathaniel Ferrer and Dr. Nelson Hora — confirmed critical gaps in health communication across their 14-barangay coverage area.

⚙️ How does it work?

The app uses a USB GSM Modem (SIM7600X) and AT Commands to send SMS directly from a dedicated health center computer. Resident data is stored in an AES-256 encrypted SQLite database — a single file that becomes completely unreadable when the app is closed. No cloud, no third-party APIs, no data leaving the facility.

💡 Why was it built?

Community residents were missing free vaccinations, check-ups, and medicine distributions because health centers relied on unreliable methods — posters, word-of-mouth, and a sporadically updated Facebook page. iKalinga SMS delivers timely, direct, and inclusive health notifications to everyone, regardless of internet access.

Features

Everything a health center needs

Designed to be simple enough for BHWs to use daily, yet robust enough to protect sensitive patient data at the highest standard.

🔐

Digital Vault (AES-256)

The entire resident database is encrypted at rest using military-grade AES-256 encryption. When the app is closed, the database becomes completely unreadable — protecting patient privacy even if the computer is stolen or lost.

📡

Offline SMS Broadcasting

Send SMS broadcasts directly through a USB GSM Modem using standard AT Commands — zero internet required. Patient phone numbers are never sent to a third-party service and never leave the health center's computer.

🎯

Targeted Group Messaging

Create custom SMS groups like "Pregnant Mothers", "Hypertensive", "Senior Citizens", or "Diabetic". Filter by Barangay, Sex, and Age Range to send the right health announcement to exactly the right people.

🛡️

RA 10173 Compliance

Mandatory, logged consent tracking for every resident — as required by the Data Privacy Act. No resident can be saved to the database without their explicit documented consent to receive SMS updates.

🔑

Role-Based Access Control

Two distinct access levels: Admin (full access to manage residents, groups, and user accounts) and Reader (send SMS broadcasts only, cannot alter records). Prevents accidental data changes.

📋

Immutable Audit Logs

Every action is tracked — who logged in, what messages were sent, who added or modified a resident record. Logs are read-only within the app and can be exported as a CSV file for compliance or health coordinator review.

👤

Two-Step Security

Access requires two sequential authentications: a Master Password (Vault Key) that decrypts the database, then a personal Username and Password. Even if one credential is compromised, data remains protected.

⚡

Power-Loss Recovery

If power is lost while the app is running, a built-in recovery process safely restores the database the next time the app is opened. Simply enter the Master Password and continue — no data is lost.

🆓

Completely Free

iKalinga SMS is a community project with no licensing fees, no subscriptions, and no hidden costs. The only ongoing cost is the SIM card's unli-text plan — typically less than ₱100/month.

How It Works

Simple for health workers, secure by design

From installation to sending your first broadcast — here's the full flow.

1

Install & Set Up

Run the installer, plug in the USB GSM Modem, and complete the one-time First-Time Setup to create your Vault Master Password and Admin account.

2

Unlock & Log In

On each use, enter the Vault Master Password to decrypt the database, then log in with your personal username and password to access your role.

3

Register Residents

Admins add resident details — Name, Phone, Barangay, Sex, Date of Birth — with their mandatory RA 10173 consent checked and logged.

4

Send Broadcasts

Select a recipient group, compose your health announcement, and click Send. The app delivers it via the USB modem — SMS by SMS, with a live progress bar.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Quick answers to the most common questions about installing, using, and troubleshooting iKalinga SMS.

What do I do when the app shows "No COM ports found" or a modem error? ▼
Unplug the USB modem, wait 5 seconds, then plug it into a different USB port. Inside the app, go to System Settings and click the Refresh (circular arrow) button. If it still doesn't appear, open Windows Device Manager and confirm the SIM7600X drivers are installed correctly — look for "SimTech AT Port" under "Ports (COM & LPT)". The COM port number listed there must match the one selected in the app.
I forgot the Vault Master Password. Can I recover my data? ▼
Unfortunately, no. The Vault Master Password is the only key to the AES-256 encrypted database. There is no backdoor and no account recovery — this is intentional to ensure maximum data privacy. We strongly recommend writing down the Master Password and storing it securely in a locked location at the health center (e.g., with the facility's administrator). If the password is permanently lost, a fresh database must be initialized and resident records re-entered.
The power went out while the app was running. Is my data safe? ▼
Yes. When you reopen the app, you will see a warning: "System recovered from unexpected shutdown." Simply enter your Vault Master Password as usual. The app has a built-in recovery process that safely restores your database from the state before the power loss. No data will be lost.
Can I use iKalinga SMS without a USB modem or SIM card? ▼
Partially. All features related to the resident database — adding residents, managing groups, editing records, viewing audit logs — work without a modem. However, to send SMS broadcasts, a physically connected USB GSM Modem with an active SIM card (unli-text plan) is required. The Messaging Hub will show a modem error if no device is detected.
Is iKalinga SMS compliant with the Data Privacy Act (RA 10173)? ▼
Yes — compliance was a core design requirement from day one. Every resident must have their explicit, documented consent recorded before being added to the database. Phone numbers never leave the health center computer and are never transmitted to any cloud service or third-party API. The database is encrypted at rest (AES-256), and all actions are permanently logged in an immutable audit trail.
Can a Reader (non-Admin) still send SMS messages? ▼
Yes. Users with the "Reader" role have full access to the Messaging Hub and can compose and send SMS broadcasts to any group. They can also view the resident directory. What they cannot do is add, edit, or delete any resident records, manage SMS groups, or create/delete user accounts — those actions are restricted to Admins only.
How do I safely close the application? ▼
Always close iKalinga SMS by clicking the standard "X" button at the top right of the window. The app will ask "Are you sure you want to exit?" — click Yes. The app will then re-encrypt the database, securely delete the temporary file, and shut down safely. Never force-close the app through Task Manager, as this may interrupt the encryption process and could require recovery on the next launch.
Can iKalinga SMS be used by health centers outside of Baguio City? ▼
Absolutely. While iKalinga SMS was initially developed and piloted at City Camp District Health Center in Baguio City, it is designed to work at any barangay-level health center in the Philippines with a Windows computer and a USB GSM Modem. The software is free. If your health center would like assistance setting it up, please reach out through the Community page on GitHub.
A staff member forgot their login password. What can we do? ▼
An Admin can resolve this easily. Go to System Settings → User Management, find the staff member's account, and delete it. Then create a new account for them with a new username and password. This does not affect any resident data or audit logs.
What modem do you recommend? ▼
The Waveshare SIM7600X 4G HAT (USB version) is the recommended modem. It supports AT Commands over a standard serial port, includes a 4G LTE antenna, and its drivers are included in the iKalinga SMS installer package. Any USB GSM Modem that uses the Hayes AT Command set and appears as a "SimTech AT Port" in Windows Device Manager should also work, but the SIM7600X has been tested and verified.
Privacy & Compliance

Built around patient privacy

iKalinga SMS was designed from the ground up to comply with the Data Privacy Act of the Philippines (Republic Act 10173).

🔒

Data Stored Locally Only

All resident data lives exclusively on the health center's computer. There are no cloud uploads, no API calls, and no third-party services involved. If the computer is offline, the app works identically.

✅

Mandatory Consent Logging

The system enforces a mandatory "Consent (RA 10173)" checkbox for every resident. If the checkbox is not checked, the system will refuse to save the record and display an explanatory message.

🗂️

Audit Trail

Every action in the system — logins, message sends, record additions and deletions — is permanently logged with a timestamp and the acting user's name. Logs are read-only inside the app.

🛡️

AES-256 Encryption at Rest

The database file is encrypted using AES-256 whenever the app is not actively running. This is the same encryption standard used in banking and government systems.

🚫

Phone Numbers Never Leave the Center

SMS messages are sent directly from the app to the USB modem using serial commands. No phone number is ever transmitted to an online gateway, a server, or any external party.

🔑

Hashed Passwords

Staff account passwords are never stored in plain text. They are stored as one-way SHA-256 hashes — even system administrators cannot read them from the database.
